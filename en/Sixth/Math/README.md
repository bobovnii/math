Note: Topics align with typical Year 6 maths in England (National Curriculum); offline environment, compiled from general knowledge.

### Number & Place Value
- Numbers to 10 000 000 and place value
  - Place Value to 10 Million (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Identify the value of a digit by its place in large numbers.
    - Example 1: Value of 6 in 6 482 159 -> 6 000 000
    - Example 2: Which digit is in the hundred thousands place of 3 947 218? -> 9
  - Compare and Order Large Numbers (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Compare from the leftmost digit, then move right.
    - Example 1: Which is greatest: 6 905 210, 6 950 210, 6 509 210? -> 6 950 210
    - Example 2: Arrange ascending: 4 320 500; 4 302 500; 4 325 000 -> 4 302 500, 4 320 500, 4 325 000
- Rounding and negative numbers
  - Round to Millions (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Look at the next digit to round to the nearest 10 000, 100 000, or million.
    - Example 1: Round 5 762 490 to the nearest 100 000 -> 5 800 000
    - Example 2: A number rounds to 3 000 000 to the nearest million. Give one -> 2 750 000
  - Negative Steps (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Use a number line to add or subtract across zero.
    - Example 1: -7 + 12 = ? -> 5
    - Example 2: Which is smaller: -18 or -12? -> -18

### Four Operations
- Multi-digit addition and subtraction
  - Column Add 3-Digit (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Align digits and carry carefully.
    - Example 1: 487 + 369 = ? -> 856
    - Example 2: 7 250 + ___ = 8 004 -> 754
  - Column Subtract 3-Digit (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Borrow when the top digit is smaller.
    - Example 1: 912 - 487 = ? -> 425
    - Example 2: ___ - 658 = 247 -> 905
- Multiplication and division
  - Short Multiplication (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Multiply by a single digit and regroup.
    - Example 1: 742 × 8 = ? -> 5 936
    - Example 2: __ × 7 = 3 164 -> 452
  - Long Multiplication (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Use partial products for 2-digit × 2- or 3-digit.
    - Example 1: 124 × 23 ≈ ? (rounding) -> about 2 800
    - Example 2: 218 × 14 = ? -> 3 052
  - Short Division with Remainders (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Divide place by place; write any remainder.
    - Example 1: 1 248 ÷ 7 = ? -> 178 R2
    - Example 2: __ ÷ 9 = 243 R6 -> 27
  - Divide by 10/100/1000 (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Move digits right for division, left for multiplication by powers of ten.
    - Example 1: 5 270 ÷ 100 = ? -> 52.7
    - Example 2: 0.846 × 1000 = ? -> 846

### Fractions, Decimals, Percentages
- Fraction comparison and calculation
  - Simplify and Compare (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Simplify then compare using common denominators or cross-multiply.
    - Example 1: Which is greater: 7/12 or 5/8? -> 7/12
    - Example 2: Simplify 18/24 -> 3/4
  - Add/Subtract Fractions (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Find common denominators before adding or subtracting.
    - Example 1: 3/4 + 2/3 = ? -> 17/12 (or 1 5/12)
    - Example 2: 7/8 - 1/6 = ? -> 17/24
  - Multiply/Divide Fractions (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Multiply across; for division multiply by the reciprocal.
    - Example 1: 2/3 × 5/6 = ? -> 10/18 (or 5/9)
    - Example 2: 3/5 ÷ 2/3 = ? -> 9/10
  - Fraction of an Amount (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Divide by the denominator, then multiply by the numerator.
    - Example 1: Find 3/5 of 250 -> 150
    - Example 2: Find 7/12 of 240 -> 140
- Decimals and percentages
  - Decimal Multiply/Divide by Whole (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Shift the decimal point; check place value.
    - Example 1: 3.46 × 7 = ? -> 24.22
    - Example 2: 58.8 ÷ 6 = ? -> 9.8
  - Percent-Decimal-Fraction (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: 10% = 0.1, 25% = 0.25 = 1/4, 50% = 0.5.
    - Example 1: Which equals 0.2? -> 20% or 1/5
    - Example 2: 45% as a fraction in simplest form -> 9/20

### Ratio and Proportion
- Scaling and simple ratio
  - Ratio Scale Up/Down (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Multiply or divide both parts by the same number.
    - Example 1: In ratio 3:4, if 3 parts = 21, find 4 parts -> 28
    - Example 2: Scale 2:5 by 3 -> 6:15
  - Recipe Proportion (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Use the scale factor on all ingredients.
    - Example 1: Recipe 2 eggs : 300 g flour. For 4 eggs, flour? -> 600 g
    - Example 2: Scale 3:2 to total 25 parts. Larger part? -> 15 parts

### Algebra (intro)
- Expressions and substitution
  - Evaluate the Expression (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Substitute values then follow order of operations.
    - Example 1: If a=3, b=5, find 2a + b -> 11
    - Example 2: If x=4, y=2, find 3x - y -> 10
  - One-Step Equations (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Use inverse operations to isolate the variable.
    - Example 1: Solve x + 7 = 19 -> 12
    - Example 2: Solve 5x = 45 -> 9
- Sequences
  - Linear Sequences (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Find the term-to-term rule.
    - Example 1: 4, 9, 14, 19, ... next? -> 24
    - Example 2: Rule 3n + 2. What is the 5th term? -> 17

### Geometry
- Angle reasoning and polygons
  - Missing Angle in Triangle/Line (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Triangle angles sum to 180°; straight line to 180°.
    - Example 1: Angles 65° and 47° in a triangle. Missing? -> 68°
    - Example 2: On a straight line, one angle is 132°. Other? -> 48°
  - Regular Polygon Angles (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Interior sum = (n-2)×180°; exterior angles total 360°.
    - Example 1: Interior angle of a regular hexagon? -> 120°
    - Example 2: Number of sides if each exterior angle is 45°? -> 8
- Coordinates and position
  - Four-Quadrant Coordinates (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Go left/right for x, up/down for y; mind signs.
    - Example 1: Coordinates of a point 3 left, 4 up -> (-3, 4)
    - Example 2: Reflect (5, -2) across the y-axis -> (-5, -2)

### Measurement
- Units and conversions
  - Metric Conversion Mix (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Multiply or divide by 10, 100, 1000 between km–m–cm–mm and kg–g.
    - Example 1: Convert 3.75 km to m -> 3 750 m
    - Example 2: Convert 450 g to kg -> 0.45 kg
- Perimeter, area, volume
  - Area of Parallelograms (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Base × height gives the area.
    - Example 1: Base 12 cm, height 8 cm. Area? -> 96 cm²
    - Example 2: Area 135 cm², height 15 cm. Base? -> 9 cm
  - Volume of Cuboids (12-button keypad)
    - Hint: Multiply length × width × height.
    - Example 1: 5 cm by 4 cm by 3 cm volume -> 60 cm³
    - Example 2: Volume 96 cm³, base 8 cm by 4 cm. Height? -> 3 cm

### Statistics
- Interpreting and drawing
  - Line Graph Stories (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: Read scales carefully and note change over time.
    - Example 1: Temperature rises from 6°C to 14°C between 8am and noon. Increase? -> 8°C
    - Example 2: A graph shows 12 km in 1.5 hours. Average speed? -> 8 km/h
  - Pie Chart Parts (6-button multiple choice)
    - Hint: 360° represents the whole; use fractions of 360 to find angles.
    - Example 1: 1/4 of a pie chart is how many degrees? -> 90°
    - Example 2: If the blue sector is 72°, what fraction is blue? -> 72/360 = 1/5
